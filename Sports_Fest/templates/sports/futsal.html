Futsal Game Rules

Game Overview

Futsal consists of two halves of 15 minutes each. The team that scores the most goals wins. Only 5 players are allowed on the pitch at a time with unlimited substitutions, which can only be made during stoppages.

Goal Scoring

  • A goal will be counted if it goes between the cones below knee height.

Throw-Ins

  • All throw-ins must be taken using the feet.

Penalties

  • All penalties are "open penalties," meaning there is no goalkeeper to defend the goal.
  • The penalty spot will be on the opposite goal.

Offside Rule

There is no offside rule in futsal.

Goalkeeper Rules

Any player on the field can act as a goalkeeper, and they can switch roles only during game stoppages.

Handball Rule

  • Players cannot deliberately handle the ball with their hands or arms. Deliberate handball results in a foul.
  • Accidental handball may not be penalized if the contact was unintentional and the player had no time to react.
  • Deliberate handball in defense, especially within the penalty area, can lead to a penalty kick for the opposing team.
  • Exceptions include situations where the ball deflects off another player or if the arms are in a natural position.
