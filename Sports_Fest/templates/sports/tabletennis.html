Objective

The objective of table tennis is to score points by hitting the ball over the net onto the opponent's side of the table in such a way that the opponent cannot make a valid return. The player or team that wins the most games wins the match.

Setup

  • Table:
    • The table measures 2.74 meters in length, 1.525 meters in width, and stands 76 centimeters high.
    • A vertical net divides the table into two equal halves.
  • Net:
    • The net assembly, including the posts, is 15.25 centimeters high and extends across the full width of the table.
  • Ball:
    • The ball is made of celluloid or similar plastic material, has a diameter of 40mm, and weighs 2.7 grams.
  • Paddles (Rackets):
    • Paddles are made of wood, covered with rubber on one or both sides, and must meet official size and material specifications.
  • Players:
    • Table tennis can be played in singles (2 players) or doubles (4 players, 2 per team).

Gameplay

  • Serve:
    • The ball must be tossed vertically at least 16 centimeters and struck so it bounces once on the server's side and once on the opponent's side.
    • In doubles, the serve must travel diagonally from the server's right-hand side to the receiver's right-hand side.
    • After every 2 points, the serve switches to the opponent. At deuce (10-10), the serve alternates after every point.
  • Rally:
    • Players must return the ball after it bounces once on their side, ensuring it clears the net and lands on the opponent's side.
    • The rally continues until a player fails to make a valid return or commits a fault.
  • Let:
    • A let is called if the serve touches the net but lands correctly in the service area. The serve is replayed.
    • Let is also called if play is interrupted by external factors.
  • Faults:
    • Failing to make a valid serve (e.g., missing the table, not tossing the ball correctly).
    • Hitting the ball out of bounds or into the net.
    • Allowing the ball to bounce more than once on your side.
    • Touching the table with your free hand or moving the table during play.

Scoring

  • Points:
    • A point is scored when the opponent fails to make a valid return, commits a fault, or allows the ball to bounce more than once.
  • Game:
    • A game is played to 11 points, but a player must win by at least 2 points.
    • If the score reaches 10-10, known as "deuce," players continue playing until one player leads by 2 points.
  • Match:
    • A match is typically the best of 5 or 7 games. The player or team that wins the majority of games wins the match.

Additional Rules

  • Service Rotation:
    • In doubles, players alternate serves every 2 points. After each game, teams switch sides and change the serving order.
    • During rallies in doubles, teammates must alternate hitting the ball.
  • Timeouts:
    • Each player or team is allowed one 1-minute timeout per game.
  • Expedite System:
    • If a game lasts more than 10 minutes (unless both players have 9 points), the expedite system is activated.
    • Under this system, players alternate serves every point, and the receiver automatically wins the point if they return the ball 13 times without the server winning the point.
  • Clothing and Equipment:
    • Players must wear clothing that contrasts with the ball color to ensure visibility.
    • Non-reflective clothing is required to prevent distractions.
