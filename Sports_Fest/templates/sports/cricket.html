Cricket Game Rules

Game Overview

Cricket will be played between two teams, each made up of 7 players. The game consists of two innings, where each team takes turns in batting and fielding/bowling. Each inning will be of 8 overs.

Batting Rules

  • Each time a batsman runs one full length of the pitch, it equals 1 run.
  • Hitting the ball to the boundary along the ground earns 4 runs.
  • Hitting the ball over the boundary on the full earns 6 runs.
  • The aim is to score as many runs as possible before getting out.
  • If a batsman hits the ball out of the campus, they will be declared out.

Bowling Rules

  • One bowler from each team can bowl a maximum of 3 overs.
  • No bowler can bowl illegally without the batsman's consent, otherwise, it will be declared a no ball.

Fielding Rules

  • Fielding team attempts to get the batsman out by hitting the wickets with the ball, catching a batsman's shot on the full, or hitting the wickets before the batsman can run to the other end of the pitch.
  • Fielders can be substituted within the team.

Out Rules

  • Hitting the wicket with the bat is considered out.
  • Players can only be retired in case of a serious injury.
  • There is no provision for declaring a playing batsman.
  • If the ball hits the sides of the lawn not designated as boundaries, it results in a single run without a change of strike.
  • There is no provision for out if the ball gets deflected by the surrounding trees.

Dead Ball Rules

If any new situation arises, the ball will be called a dead ball.

Dispute Resolution

All disputes will be resolved through mutual consensus.
